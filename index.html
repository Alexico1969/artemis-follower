ARTEMIS II

Humanity Returns to Lunar Space

The first crewed flight of NASA's Orion spacecraft and Space Launch System. Four astronauts will travel beyond Earth orbit for the first time since Apollo 17 in 1972 — looping around the Moon and returning safely home.

10-Day Flight Plan

Mission Timeline

From liftoff at Kennedy Space Center to Pacific Ocean splashdown — a complete day-by-day breakdown of the Artemis II mission profile.

🚀
Days 0 – 1
Launch & Earth Orbit
SLS lifts off from LC-39B at Kennedy Space Center. The core stage and solid rocket boosters separate roughly 8 minutes after liftoff. Orion's interim cryogenic propulsion stage places the spacecraft into a high Earth orbit, where initial systems checks begin.
SLS Launch Earth Orbit Booster Separation
🔧
Days 1 – 2
Systems Checkout
Crew and mission control conduct thorough checkout of all critical systems. Life support, Environmental Control & Life Support (ECLSS), navigation computers, and communications arrays are all validated before departure from Earth's vicinity.
Life Support Navigation Comms Validation
🔥
Days 2 – 3
Translunar Injection (TLI)
The engine performs a Translunar Injection burn, accelerating Orion out of Earth orbit and onto a trajectory toward the Moon. This critical maneuver commits the crew to the lunar journey — there is no turning back once TLI is complete.
TLI Burn Critical Maneuver
🌌
Days 3 – 5
Transit to the Moon
Orion coasts through deep space toward the Moon. Continuous monitoring of all systems is conducted. The crew performs daily health checks, experiments, and human performance assessments in the deep-space radiation environment.
Deep Space Transit Human Research
🌕
Day 6 — KEY MOMENT
Lunar Flyby & Free Return
Orion makes its closest approach to the lunar surface. The crew will witness the Moon up close — a historic sight for the first time since Apollo. The spacecraft uses the Moon's gravity in a "free-return trajectory," naturally slingshotting back toward Earth without requiring another engine burn.
Closest Approach Gravity Assist Free Return Trajectory
📡
Days 6 – 8
Return Journey
On the homeward leg, mission controllers focus on deep-space operations testing. Human performance and radiation exposure data are collected. This phase simulates conditions that future Artemis crews will face on longer lunar surface missions.
Deep Space Ops Health Monitoring
🌊
Days 9 – 10
Reentry & Splashdown
Orion reenters Earth's atmosphere at approximately 25,000 mph — the fastest reentry of a crewed spacecraft in decades — generating temperatures around 5,000°F on the heat shield. Parachutes deploy to slow descent. The crew splashes down in the Pacific Ocean, where the recovery team retrieves the capsule.
~25,000 mph Reentry Pacific Splashdown Recovery Ops

The People

Artemis II Crew

Four astronauts representing NASA and the Canadian Space Agency — the first humans to travel beyond low Earth orbit in over 50 years.

👨‍🚀
Reid Wiseman
Commander
NASA
U.S. Navy test pilot and NASA astronaut. Chief of the Astronaut Office before being named Artemis II commander. Flew to the ISS on Soyuz TMA-13M in 2014.
👨‍🚀
Victor Glover
Pilot
NASA
U.S. Navy test pilot and NASA astronaut. Served as Pilot on SpaceX Crew Dragon Resilience to the ISS in 2020 and was the first Black astronaut to serve as a long-duration crew member on the station.
👩‍🚀
Christina Koch
Mission Specialist 1
NASA
Holds the record for the longest single spaceflight by a woman (328 days). Participated in the first all-female spacewalk. Will be the first woman to fly to lunar distance.
👨‍🚀
Jeremy Hansen
Mission Specialist 2
CSA · Canada
Canadian Space Agency astronaut and Royal Canadian Air Force colonel. Will be the first Canadian — and first non-American — to travel to lunar distance. Has not previously flown to space.

The Big Picture

Artemis Program Timeline

NASA's multi-mission roadmap to return humans to the Moon — and lay the groundwork for eventual crewed missions to Mars.

I
Artemis I
November 2022
Uncrewed test flight of SLS and Orion. Traveled 268,553 miles beyond Earth, performed a distant retrograde orbit of the Moon, and splashed down successfully after a 25.5-day mission.
✓ Completed
II
Artemis II
2026
First crewed Artemis flight. Four astronauts on a 10-day mission around the Moon and back. Validates Orion's life support systems and crew operations in deep space.
⬤ This Mission
III
Artemis III
~2027+
First crewed lunar landing since Apollo 17 (1972). Astronauts will land at the lunar South Pole using SpaceX Starship as the Human Landing System. Includes first woman on the Moon.
Planned
IV
Artemis IV
~2028+
Expanded lunar surface operations and assembly of Gateway — a small space station in lunar orbit. Sets the stage for sustained human presence in the Earth-Moon system.
Planned
